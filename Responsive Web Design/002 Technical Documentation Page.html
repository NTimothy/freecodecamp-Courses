Introduction

This page summarizes step-by-step instructions provided in the iHealth COVID-19 Antigen Rapid At-Home Test kit [Model ICO-3000/ICO-30001/ICO-30002]. By following the instructions on the page, it is possible to obtain results in approximately 15 minutes. It is important to note that, while this particular model has neither been cleared nor approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA), it has been authorized under an Emergency Use Authorization.

Prepare Materials

TEST SET #1:

  • 1 x COVID-19 Test Card in Pouch
  • 1 x Pre-filled Tube
  • 1 x Swab

Open the package, take out the COVID-19 Test Card in the pouch, the Tube prefilled with the extraction solution, and the Swab. Since the tube has already been pre-filled for your convenience, you may proceed to the next step.

TEST SET #2:

  • 1 x COVID-19 Test Card in Pouch
  • 1 x Empty Tube
  • 1 x Sealed Solution
  • 1 x Swab


Open the package, take out the COVID-19 Test Card in Pouch, empty Tube, sealed Solution and the Swab. When you are ready to proceed with the test, open the foil pouch of the COVID-19 Test Card.

Collect Sample

  1. Remove the swab from its pacakage, being careful not to touch the tip of the swab. Please keep the swab package for later use.
  2. Gently insert the entire absorbent tip of the swab (usually 1/2 to 3/4 of an inch) into your nostril.
    Note: With children, the maximum depth of insertion may be less than 3/4 of an inch, and you may need to have a second person to hold the child's head while swabbing

  3. Firmly and slowly brush against insides of nostril in a circular motion against the nasal wall at least 5 times. Take at least 15 seconds to collect the specimen and be sure to collect any nasal drainage on the swab. Using both the same swab, repeat the same sample collection procedure for the other nostril. Be sure to brush BOTH nostrils with the SAME SWAB.
    Note: Failure to swab properly may cause false negative results.

Prepare Sample

  1. Tape the tube vertically on the table and twist the large orange cap to open the tube.
  2. Insert the swab into the tube, touch the bottom of the tube with the swab tip, and stir at least 15 times
  3. Squeeze the sides of the tube to express how much liquid as possible from the swab, and then remove the swab.
    If you don't squeeze the swab, there may not be sufficient sample material to perform the test properly (i.e., protentially resulting in a false negative result).
  4. Screw back the large orange cap, put the swab back into the package. Safely dispose of the swab and the package.

Test Sample

  1. Twist to open the small white cap of the tube. Add 3 drops of sample to the Sample Port of the COVID-19 Test Card. Screw back the small white cap.
    Note: A false negative or invalid result may occur if too little solution is added to the test card.
  2. Start a timer and wait for 15 minutes. You may use a timer or a clock to ensure the appropriate amount of time has passed.
    Note: Do NOT interpret your test result until after 15 minutes have passed, the T line may take as long as 15 minutes to appear.
  3. After 15 minutes have passed, you may read your result. Results should NOT be read after 30 minutes.
    Note: A false negative or false positive result may occur if the test result is read before 15 minutes or after 30 minutes.

Results Explanation

  1. Positive Result

    A positive result must show BOTH a C line and a T line. A positive result means that viral antigens from COVID-19 were detected and the individual is positive for COVID-19.

    Persons who test positive should self-isolate and sek follow-up care with their physician or healthcare provider as additional testing and public health reporting may be necessary

  2. Negative Result

    A NEGATIVE result will ONLY show a C line. A negative result means that viral antigens from COVID-19 were not detected and that the individual is presumed negative for COVID-19.

Sample Disposal

After the test is completed, dispose of the kit and all components into the trash.
